## Introduction

This paper examines the legal and constitutional foundations for treating self-custodied Bitcoin as sui generis bearer property, with particular focus on property rights and privacy protections. It provides doctrinal support for the positions taken in the broader Bitcoin Sovereignty Research Framework.

## 1. Property Rights Foundations

Under both natural law and positive constitutional law, property rights are fundamental. Self-custodied Bitcoin satisfies the core requirements of property:

- **Just acquisition**: Through homesteading via proof-of-work energy expenditure.
- **Exclusive control**: Exercised through private keys/seed phrases.
- **Transferability**: Through voluntary transactions on the blockchain.

Because control can reside entirely in the mind, Bitcoin represents a form of property that is uniquely portable and resistant to physical seizure without compelled disclosure.

## 2. Privacy of Mind and Cryptographic Control

The ability to control Bitcoin through memorised seed phrases engages fundamental privacy protections:

- **Mental privacy**: Seed phrases exist as thoughts or knowledge in the mind.
- **Freedom of thought**: Compelled disclosure of mental content violates freedom of thought.
- **Privilege against self-incrimination**: In many jurisdictions, being forced to reveal a seed phrase can be viewed as compelled testimony.

These protections are engaged whether the seed phrase is memorised or stored in secure offline form.

## 3. Constitutional Anchors (Comparative)

While specific constitutional provisions vary by jurisdiction, common principles include:

- Right to property
- Right to privacy
- Freedom of expression / speech
- Privilege against self-incrimination
- Protection against unreasonable search and seizure

Self-custodied Bitcoin sits at the intersection of these protections in a way that traditional financial assets do not.

## 4. Implications for Regulation

Regulatory frameworks that:
- Treat mere holding as a reportable or taxable event
- Require disclosure of private keys or seed phrases
- Impose capital controls on self-custodied Bitcoin

...risk violating these foundational legal principles.

## 5. Conclusion

The sui generis nature of self-custodied Bitcoin is not merely a policy preference — it is grounded in deep legal and constitutional principles regarding property and privacy. Any coherent regulatory approach must start from this recognition.
