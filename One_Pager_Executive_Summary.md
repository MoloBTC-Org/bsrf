### Core Thesis

Bitcoin self-custody is the highest-assurance configuration for monetary sovereignty. It materially reduces counterparty, surveillance, and institutional freeze risk compared to custodial models, while converting remaining operational risk into manageable, documented practice.

### The Two-Tier Structure

**Tier 1 – Foundational Papers**  
Philosophical, jurisprudential, and strategic foundations:
- Bitcoin as Sui Generis Bearer Property
- Bitcoin as Adjacent Currency under International Law
- Bitcoin Self-Custody as Sovereign Monetary Infrastructure (synthesis)

**Tier 2 – Operational Companions**  
Historical patterns, risk framework, and practical implementation:
- Centralised Monetary Governance
- Strategic Risk-Mitigation Framework
- Critical Monetary Infrastructure

### Key Insight

Centralised monetary governance has historically expanded control during periods of stress. Self-custody, built on open protocols and energy security, provides a structural counter to these patterns.
