## Executive Summary

Bitcoin, particularly in self-custodied form, constitutes a *sui generis* bearer asset and **adjacent currency** that operates beyond the territorial monetary and tax categories of any single sovereign. It exists as cryptographically secured value in the global layer of mathematics, energy expenditure (proof-of-work), and individual knowledge (memorized seed phrases).

Its borderless, protocol-enforced nature creates a fundamental mismatch with traditional international tax and jurisdiction rules designed for physical assets, central issuers, or intermediary-dependent financial instruments.

Under international law principles of sovereignty, jurisdiction, and human rights, states possess limited legitimate authority to tax or control an asset whose existence and security require no unique state infrastructure and whose ultimate control resides in protected personal thought.

The appropriate global response is recognition of **adjacent currency status** coupled with separate local taxation limited to realized economic activity within each sovereign’s jurisdiction, coordinated through principles analogous to Double Tax Agreements (DTAs).

---

## 1. Introduction: Bitcoin’s Challenge to Territorial Jurisdiction and Tax Categories

International law has long grappled with the allocation of jurisdiction over persons, property, and economic activity across borders. Classical principles — territoriality, nationality, protective principle, and effects doctrine — were developed in an era of physical assets, central monetary issuers, and intermediary-dependent finance.

Bitcoin, especially self-custodied Bitcoin, disrupts these assumptions at a fundamental level:

- A self-custodied bitcoin UTXO can be controlled from anywhere with internet access (or even offline via memorized seed).
- Its value moves without physical tokens or human bodies crossing borders.
- Its security derives from global proof-of-work energy competition and cryptographic mathematics.
- Its highest form of control resides in human memory — protected thought.

### 1.1 The Core Thesis

Bitcoin (self-custodied) should be treated internationally as an **adjacent currency** — a parallel, non-sovereign medium of exchange and store of value that coexists with national currencies without claiming legal tender status within any state.

Sovereigns should coordinate, through principles analogous to Double Tax Agreements, to allocate taxing rights solely over realized economic activity occurring within their jurisdiction, while agreeing that the asset itself (holding, pure movement, and self-custody) lies outside the tax base.

---

## 2. Sui Generis Bearer Property Confirmed

Self-custodied Bitcoin satisfies the criteria for *sui generis* bearer property under both Natural Law and positive international law analogies:

- It is rivalrous and excludable through cryptographic control (private keys/seed phrases).
- It is acquired through homesteading via irreversible energy expenditure (proof-of-work) and intellectual labour.
- It requires no central issuer, registry, or ongoing state protection for its existence or transfer.
- Its ultimate control layer can exist purely as protected personal knowledge.
- Its protocol rules are enforced by mathematics and global energy markets, not by any territorial sovereign.

---

## 3. Jurisdiction under International Law

The traditional bases of jurisdiction have limited application to self-custodied Bitcoin:

- **Territoriality**: The asset has no fixed physical location; UTXOs exist on a globally replicated ledger.
- **Nationality**: The holder may be a national, but the asset’s security and transfer do not depend on that nationality or on state protection.
- **Effects/Protective**: While Bitcoin can affect local markets, the protocol itself produces global public goods without consuming dedicated state resources.

Compelled disclosure of memorized seed phrases or private keys raises additional international human rights concerns under instruments such as the International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights (privacy and fair trial protections).

---

## 4. Double Tax Agreements as the Appropriate Model for Coordination

Double Tax Agreements (DTAs) represent the international community’s most successful mechanism for coordinating taxing rights across borders while preserving sovereignty. They allocate taxing rights between contracting states and provide mechanisms to relieve double taxation — without creating a global tax authority or imposing uniform rules.

Applying the DTA analogy to Bitcoin yields a clear principle:

> Taxing rights over realized gains from economic activity should be allocated to the jurisdiction(s) with the strongest connection to that activity, while the asset itself (holding, movement, and self-custody) should be treated as outside the tax base.

This is **coordination, not harmonization**. States remain free to set their own rates and definitions, provided they respect the allocation principles and the *sui generis* exclusion of the bearer asset layer.

## 5. Principles for Adjacent Currency Status and Separate Local Realization Taxation

### 5.1 Adjacent Currency Recognition

Bitcoin should be recognized as an “adjacent currency” — legally distinct from both domestic legal tender and from conventional foreign currency for tax and regulatory purposes.

### 5.2 Separate Local Realization Taxation

Taxing rights should be limited to realized economic gains arising from voluntary activity with a sufficient connection to the taxing jurisdiction. Pure holding of self-custodied Bitcoin, cross-border transfers that do not involve local realization, and self-custody itself should remain outside the tax base.

### 5.3 Double-Tax Coordination

States should coordinate, bilaterally or multilaterally, to allocate taxing rights on realized Bitcoin gains according to principles analogous to DTAs and to provide relief from double taxation where multiple jurisdictions claim rights.

### 5.4 Human Rights Safeguards

Any enforcement measures, including compelled disclosure of keys or seeds, must comply with international human rights standards regarding privacy, freedom of thought, property, and fair trial. The mind-resident character of ultimate self-custody control creates a heightened threshold for justification of coercive measures.

## 6. Natural Law and Austrian Reinforcement in the International Sphere

The international law analysis is reinforced by the foundations established in *Bitcoin as Sui Generis Bearer Property*:

- **Natural Law** supplies the rights-based limits on state power over memorized control information and justly acquired bearer property.
- **Austrian economics** demonstrates why taxing the emergent sound-money medium itself is economically incoherent and unnecessary.

Together, these frameworks explain why the global case for adjacent currency status and separate local realization taxation is not merely pragmatic but principled.

---

## 7. The Global Case

The global case rests on four converging grounds:

1. **Jurisdictional Reality** — Bitcoin’s essential characteristics place fundamental limits on what territorial states can effectively and legitimately tax or control.
2. **Rights Consistency** — Recognizing *sui generis* status is consistent with international human rights obligations.
3. **Economic Soundness** — Taxing the medium at the holding or movement layer undermines sound money functions without providing offsetting benefits.
4. **Sovereignty Preservation with Coordination** — The DTA model allows states to retain full monetary and tax sovereignty while coordinating to avoid double taxation and harmful jurisdictional conflicts.

---

## 8. Recommended International Principles

**Principle 1 — Adjacent Currency Recognition**  
Self-custodied Bitcoin shall be recognized as an adjacent currency — a *sui generis* bearer medium operating in the global protocol layer.

**Principle 2 — Exclusion of the Asset Layer from the Tax Base**  
The mere holding, cross-border movement, or self-custody of Bitcoin shall not constitute a taxable event.

**Principle 3 — Allocation of Taxing Rights on Realized Activity**  
Taxing rights over realized gains shall be allocated between states according to principles analogous to Double Tax Agreements.

**Principle 4 — Human Rights Compliance in Enforcement**  
Any measures to compel disclosure of private keys or seed phrases must be justified under applicable international human rights standards.

## 9. Truth & Clarity Summary

**Internal Logical Coherence:** High (~88%)  
**Real-World Alignment:** Partial and emerging (~40–55%) — a growing number of states treat crypto as property and apply realization-based taxation, but no comprehensive international instrument yet recognizes *sui generis* adjacent currency status.  
**Overall Assessment:** This whitepaper provides a coherent, principled international law foundation for the treatment of self-custodied Bitcoin as adjacent currency with separate local realization taxation.
