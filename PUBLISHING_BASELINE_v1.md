# Bitcoin Sovereignty Publishing Baseline v1.0

**Status**: Authoritative and Protected  
**Applies to**: All documents in the Bitcoin Sovereignty Research Framework repository

## 1. Purpose

This Baseline establishes the minimum professional, structural, and intellectual standards for all documents published in this repository.

## 2. Core Thesis (Mandatory Alignment)

All documents must be consistent with the following positions:

- Bitcoin is **sui generis bearer property**
- Bitcoin functions as an **adjacent currency** to state-issued money
- Self-custody is the highest expression of control over Bitcoin
- The **Right to Sovereign Private Compute** is a fundamental extension of privacy and property rights
- There must be a clear distinction between Bitcoin and issuer-dependent crypto assets
- Taxation and regulatory treatment should apply only to realised economic activity with a sufficient territorial nexus
- Where regulations are unclear or in transition, temporary protections (including amnesty and grace periods) should apply to good-faith actors

Documents that contradict these positions will not be accepted.

## 3. Document Structure Requirements

Every document must include:

1. Title
2. Version number (e.g. v1.0)
3. Published by line (MoloBTC)
4. License reference (BSOL v1.0)
5. Executive Summary
6. Clear section headings
7. Conclusion or Truth & Clarity Summary
8. Cross-references to related documents where appropriate

## 4. Tone and Language

- Professional, precise, and rigorous
- Avoid hype, marketing language, or ideological slogans
- Use clear legal and policy terminology
- Maintain intellectual honesty

## 5. Document Placement

Documents should be placed in the appropriate folder:

- **Tier 1 Foundations** → `foundations/`
- **Tier 2 Operational** → `operational/`
- **Jurisdiction-specific analysis** → `jurisdictions/`
- **Policy and templates** → `policy/`
- **Supporting materials** → `supporting/`

## 6. Versioning

- Major documents use semantic versioning (`_v1.0.md`, `_v1.1.md`)
- Significant changes to core positions require a version increment
- Minor formatting edits may use date stamps

## 7. Review Process

New documents are subject to review against this Baseline. The Publishing Baseline itself is a protected document. Changes require explicit maintainer approval.
