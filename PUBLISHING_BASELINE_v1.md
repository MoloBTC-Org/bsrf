# Bitcoin Sovereignty Publishing Baseline v1

**Status**: Authoritative and Protected  
**Applies to**: All documents in the Bitcoin Sovereignty Research Framework repository

## 1. Purpose

This Baseline establishes the minimum professional, structural, and intellectual standards for all documents published in this repository. It ensures consistency, auditability, and alignment with the core thesis of the framework.

## 2. Core Thesis (Mandatory Alignment)

All documents must be consistent with the following positions:

- Bitcoin is **sui generis bearer property** — control can reside entirely in the mind via seed phrases, and acquisition occurs through proof-of-work homesteading.
- Bitcoin functions as an **adjacent currency** to state-issued money.
- Self-custody is the highest expression of control over Bitcoin.
- The **Right to Sovereign Private Compute** — the right to operate open-source software, including Bitcoin nodes, on personal devices without interference — is a fundamental extension of privacy, property, and freedom of expression.
- There must be a clear distinction between Bitcoin and issuer-dependent crypto assets.
- Taxation and regulatory treatment should be limited to realised economic activity with a sufficient territorial nexus.
- Where regulations are unclear or in transition, temporary protections (including amnesty and grace periods) should apply to good-faith actors.

Documents that contradict these positions will not be accepted.

## 3. Document Structure Requirements

Every document must include:

1. **Title**
2. **Version number** (e.g., v1.0)
3. **Published by** line (MoloBTC)
4. **License** reference (BSOL v1.0)
5. **Executive Summary** (2–5 paragraphs)
6. **Clear section headings**
7. **Conclusion** or **Truth & Clarity Summary**
8. **Cross-references** to related documents where appropriate

## 4. Tone and Language

- Professional, precise, and rigorous.
- Avoid hype, marketing language, or ideological slogans.
- Use clear legal and policy terminology where appropriate.
- Maintain intellectual honesty — acknowledge limitations and competing views where relevant.

## 5. Referencing and Citations

- Use consistent citation style (author, title, date, jurisdiction where applicable).
- Prefer primary sources (court judgments, statutes, official guidance) over secondary commentary.
- Maintain a consolidated references document where possible.

## 6. Versioning

- Major documents use semantic versioning (`_v1.0.md`, `_v1.1.md`, etc.).
- Changes to core positions or significant structural updates require a version increment.
- Minor formatting or clarification edits may use patch versions or simple date stamps.

## 7. Two-Tier Model

Documents should be placed in the appropriate tier:

- **Tier 1 (`foundations/`)**: Philosophical, jurisprudential, and historical foundations.
- **Tier 2 (`operational/`)**: Risk, implementation, infrastructure, and practical guidance.

Cross-referencing between tiers is encouraged.

## 8. Templates

All new documents should be created using the approved templates located in the `templates/` folder.

## 9. Review Process

- New documents are subject to review against this Baseline.
- Maintainers may request revisions for clarity, consistency, or alignment.
- The Publishing Baseline itself is a protected document. Changes require explicit approval.

## 10. Non-Compliance

Documents that significantly deviate from this Baseline may be:
- Returned for revision, or
- Rejected from the repository.
