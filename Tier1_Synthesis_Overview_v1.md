## Wamkelekile / Welcome

This document serves as the front door to the Tier 1 foundational papers within the Bitcoin Self-Custody Research Ecosystem. Its purpose is to help readers quickly understand the structure and find the most relevant entry point for their needs. ### Purpose of Tier 1

Tier 1 establishes the core philosophical, jurisprudential, and strategic foundations for understanding Bitcoin as *sui generis* bearer property and self-custody as sovereign monetary infrastructure. These papers provide the conceptual grounding upon which the operational guidance in Tier 2 is built.

### Document Structure – Tier 1

Tier 1 consists of three foundational papers designed to be read as an integrated set:

1. **Bitcoin as Sui Generis Bearer Property** — The core jurisprudential foundation establishing Bitcoin’s unique legal character under Natural Law and Austrian economics.
2. **Bitcoin as Adjacent Currency under International Law** — Analysis of jurisdiction, tax treatment, double-tax coordination, and the positioning of self-custodied Bitcoin in the international system.
3. **Bitcoin Self-Custody as Sovereign Monetary Infrastructure** — The central integrative synthesis connecting historical patterns of monetary governance, organisational risk, and practical implementation pathways (the primary hub document).

## The Three Tier 1 Foundational Papers

### 1. Bitcoin as Sui Generis Bearer Property

**Role:** Core jurisprudential and economic foundation.

This paper establishes that self-custodied Bitcoin constitutes *sui generis* bearer property. It demonstrates, under Natural Law and Austrian economics, that there is no coherent moral or economic foundation for taxing the asset itself (holding, movement, or unrealised appreciation). Taxation remains legitimate only when limited to realised economic activity at observable interfaces with state-favoured monetary systems.

**Read the full expansive version:**  
`Tier1_Sui_Generis_Bearer_Property_v1.md`

---

### 2. Bitcoin as Adjacent Currency under International Law

**Role:** International legal and tax coordination framework.

This paper extends the *sui generis* analysis into the international domain. It argues that self-custodied Bitcoin functions as an **adjacent currency** — a parallel, non-sovereign medium that operates beyond the territorial monetary and tax categories of any single state. It proposes coordination of taxing rights over realised activity through mechanisms analogous to Double Tax Agreements.

**Read the full expansive version:**  
`Tier1_Adjacent_Currency_v1.md`

---

### 3. Bitcoin Self-Custody as Sovereign Monetary Infrastructure

**Role:** Central integrative hub document.

This is the primary hub paper of Tier 1. It connects the jurisprudential foundations with historical patterns of centralised monetary governance and a strategic risk-mitigation framework. It shows how self-custody converts institutional and political risk into manageable operational risk, restoring a meaningful degree of monetary sovereignty at the organisational level.

**Read the full expansive version:**  
`Tier1_Sovereign_Monetary_Infrastructure_v1.md`

## How the Three Papers Work Together

The three foundational papers form a coherent progression:

- **Sui Generis Bearer Property** → Supplies the rights-based and economic justification
- **Adjacent Currency under International Law** → Extends the argument into global legal and tax coordination
- **Sovereign Monetary Infrastructure** → Synthesises both into strategic and historical application for organisations

Together they provide the philosophical, legal, and strategic “why” that underpins the practical “how” developed in Tier 2.
