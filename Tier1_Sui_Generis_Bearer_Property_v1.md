## Executive Summary

Under Natural Law and Austrian economics, self-custodied Bitcoin constitutes *sui generis* bearer property and economic speech. It arises spontaneously through purposeful energy expenditure (proof-of-work), exists as memorised knowledge or cryptographically signed packets, and requires no dedicated state infrastructure or intermediaries for its existence, security, or transfer. Because the state provides no unique service or protection for the asset itself, broad taxation on mere holding, movement, or unrealised appreciation lacks logical, moral, or economic foundation.

Taxation remains coherent only when limited to realised profits arising from voluntary economic activity at observable interfaces with state-favoured monetary systems, above meaningful thresholds, and through normal reporting. Any attempt to tax or restrict the asset *qua* asset necessarily targets the individual holder and requires coercion at the human endpoint, colliding with self-ownership, privacy of thought, and the non-aggression principle.

### Why Austrian Economics as the Core Focus?

While Natural Law provides the foundational rights architecture (self-ownership, homesteading via labour-mixing, non-aggression), Austrian economics supplies the superior practical and explanatory framework for policy. It explains Bitcoin’s emergence as living proof of spontaneous order, demonstrates why centralised monetary planning fails when applied to emergent digital scarcity, and offers praxeological tools that translate directly into clear policy distinctions between taxing activity versus taxing the medium itself.

This whitepaper delivers a focused, deductive case stripped of extraneous material, preserving protocol invariants (exact 21,000,000 supply cap, energy-secured consensus, seed phrases as mind-resident property). It is designed for legislative submissions, judicial reference, academic scrutiny, and individual sovereignty structuring.

## 1. Introduction: The Anomaly of Emergent Digital Scarcity and State Taxation Claims

Positive law in most jurisdictions continues to wrestle with the categorisation of cryptocurrencies. Bitcoin in particular resists legacy boxes: it is neither conventional currency issued by a sovereign, nor a security representing a claim on future cash flows, nor a simple commodity like gold held in physical custody.

Self-custodied Bitcoin exists as cryptographically signed data packets (or their offline verbal/written equivalents) secured by real-world energy expenditure and verifiable by anyone running the protocol. Its highest-security form — a 12- or 24-word seed phrase — can reside entirely within a competent adult’s mind, air-gapped from all networks and devices.

This creates a profound anomaly for taxation systems built on assumptions of centralised ledgers, intermediaries, and physical or institutional chokepoints.

### 1.1 The Surprising Fact (Abductive Point of Departure)

The surprising fact is not merely that Bitcoin exists, but that a decentralised, energy-secured, mind-resident bearer asset has emerged and persisted without any central issuer, without dedicated state infrastructure, and without collapsing into chaos — while simultaneously exposing the enforcement limits of every territorial tax authority.

## 2. Natural Law Foundations

Natural Law rests on three interlocking pillars: self-ownership, homesteading through purposeful labour-mixing, and the non-aggression principle.

### 2.1 Self-Ownership as the Root Premise

Every competent adult is the rightful owner of their own body and mind. This is demonstrated performatively: any denial of self-ownership in argumentation presupposes the arguer’s ownership of their own cognitive faculties.

Self-ownership extends naturally to the products of one’s labour and to external resources one homesteads by mixing labour with unowned or abandoned matter.

### 2.2 Homesteading Digital Scarcity via Proof-of-Work

Bitcoin’s scarcity is produced by real energy expenditure. Miners compete to solve computationally intensive puzzles; the successful miner is rewarded with newly issued bitcoin. This is literal homesteading: unowned digital space is claimed through irreversible energy investment.

A 12- or 24-word BIP-39 seed phrase can be memorised with no physical backup remaining. The bearer property exists as pure intellectual content — the ultimate air-gapped, non-revocable, non-seizable form of property short of the mind itself.

### 2.3 Non-Aggression and the Limits of Legitimate Coercion

Because self-custodied Bitcoin is justly acquired through homesteading and requires no ongoing state protection, any non-voluntary extraction or restriction constitutes aggression. Taxation of the asset itself crosses this line.

> “The state provides no unique service or protection for the asset itself.”

This is a protocol-level observation. Bitcoin’s security model operates globally and permissionlessly. Enforcement power of any territorial state terminates at the human layer.

## 3. Austrian Economics as the Integrative Core

Austrian economics provides the explanatory “why” and the practical “how” for policy.

Bitcoin validates Mises’ regression theorem in compressed time and demonstrates Hayek’s spontaneous order in the monetary domain. Taxing the medium itself distorts its function as sound money and lacks quid-pro-quo justification.

The two frameworks reinforce each other: Natural Law explains why compelled extraction at the mind layer is rights-violating; Austrian economics explains why such extraction is also economically destructive.

## 4. Sui Generis Technical Status

Bitcoin is cryptographically signed internet packets secured by energy and mathematics. Value can be transmitted without any physical token or human body crossing a jurisdictional boundary.

The protocol invariants (SHA-256 proof-of-work, difficulty adjustment, 21,000,000 hard cap) are enforced by mathematics and global energy markets — not by any government. No state can alter the supply cap or reverse a settled transaction without sustained hashrate dominance.

Enforcement power therefore terminates at the human layer.

## 5. Privacy of Mind, Freedom of Thought, and the Non-Aggression Principle

Access to self-custodied Bitcoin in its highest-security form requires only recalling 12 or 24 words. Those words constitute private thought.

Compelled disclosure of seed phrases or private keys is equivalent to forcing a person to testify against themselves by revealing the contents of their mind — a violation of both Natural Law and many positive-law traditions (right to silence, privilege against self-incrimination).

---

## 6. The Profit-versus-Money Distinction

Modern tax systems tax *profits* or realised value expansion arising from economic activity — not money itself at the point of use or movement.

Bitcoin, functioning as an independent adjacent currency and bearer medium with its own sound-money properties, should receive equivalent treatment: no tax on holding, movement, or personal use; taxation only on measurable realised profits at observable interfaces with state-favoured systems.

---

## 7. Policy Recommendations

**Normative Claim:** There is no moral foundation for taxing the mere holding, movement, or unrealised appreciation of self-custodied Bitcoin.

**Reformist/Policy Claim:** Positive law ought to be reformed or interpreted to exempt self-custodied Bitcoin from taxation on the asset itself.

### Model Legislative Language (Example)

> “Self-custodied Bitcoin, defined as bitcoin controlled exclusively through private keys or seed phrases known solely to the beneficial owner and not held through any third-party custodian, shall be exempt from capital gains taxation on unrealised appreciation and from any capital control restrictions on its movement or transfer. Taxation shall apply only to realised gains arising from the exchange of such bitcoin for fiat currency or other assets at observable market interfaces, above a de minimis annual threshold...”

## 8. Broader Implications

Recognition of self-custodied Bitcoin as *sui generis* bearer property reinforces monetary sovereignty at the individual level and disciplines monetary authorities through credible exit options.

Sound individual property rights in high-quality money are prerequisites for communal flourishing, not antithetical to it.
