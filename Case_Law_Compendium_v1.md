## Purpose

This document serves as a living compendium of significant court rulings involving Bitcoin and other crypto assets, with particular focus on cases relevant to self-custody, property rights, taxation, and regulatory classification.

## Notable Cases

### South Africa

**Standard Bank of South Africa v South African Reserve Bank [2025] ZAGPPHC 481 (15 May 2025)**  
- Court held that cryptocurrency does not constitute “money” or “capital” under the Exchange Control Regulations, 1961.
- Emphasised the decentralised and intangible nature of crypto.
- Ruling remains subject to appeal.

**Mangundhla and Another v South African Reserve Bank (1 June 2026)**  
- Court held that Bitcoin constitutes both “money” and “capital” for exchange control purposes.
- Explicitly departed from the *Standard Bank* decision, describing it as “clearly wrong”.
- Significant implications for capital flow regulation.

### Other Jurisdictions (Summary)

- **United States**: Various cases on whether Bitcoin is money, property, or a security (e.g., SEC v. Ripple, various tax cases).
- **United Kingdom**: Cases on crypto as property for insolvency and tracing purposes.
- **European Union**: Growing body of case law under MiCA implementation and national implementations.
