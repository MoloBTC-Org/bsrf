## Executive Summary

The Netherlands is moving toward a significant shift in its tax treatment of assets, including crypto. Under current rules, crypto is taxed under Box 3 (wealth tax) based on deemed returns. Major reforms planned for 2028 will introduce taxation on actual returns, including unrealised gains for certain assets.

This development makes the Netherlands a key jurisdiction to watch regarding wealth-style taxation of Bitcoin and the tension with sui generis principles.

---

## 1. Historical Thread

The Netherlands has historically had a relatively pragmatic approach to crypto within the EU context. It has been active in EU discussions on MiCA and tax policy. The current Box 3 system (deemed return taxation) has been subject to legal challenges, leading to ongoing reform efforts.

---

## 2. Current Regulatory Overview

- **Current Tax System (until 2027)**: Crypto is taxed under Box 3 as “other investments” based on a deemed yield, regardless of actual returns.
- **2028 Reform**: The Netherlands plans to shift toward taxation on actual returns. Proposals have included elements of unrealised gains taxation, which would represent a significant departure from realisation-based principles.
- **Self-custody**: Not directly restricted.
- **Regulatory Oversight**: Intermediaries are subject to MiCA licensing and AML requirements via the AFM.

---

## 3. Key Players

- Dutch Tax Authority (Belastingdienst)
- Ministry of Finance
- Netherlands Authority for the Financial Markets (AFM)

---

## 4. Platforms for Advocacy

- Public consultations on Box 3 reform
- Engagement with Parliament and Ministry of Finance
- Industry and Bitcoin advocacy groups in the Netherlands

---

## 5. Philosophical & Legal Synthesis

The planned 2028 changes represent a move toward wealth-style taxation that conflicts with the sui generis and realisation-based framework. Strong advocacy is needed to protect self-custodied Bitcoin from unrealised gains taxation and to maintain clear distinctions between different types of digital assets.

The Netherlands’ legal tradition of property rights protection provides a foundation for arguing against overly broad wealth taxation of mind-resident assets like Bitcoin.

---

## 6. Adoption Implications

The Netherlands is currently one of the more concerning jurisdictions in Europe regarding future wealth-style taxation of crypto. The outcome of the 2028 reforms will be closely watched by self-custody advocates across Europe.

---

## Next Steps

1. Monitor the final design of the 2028 Box 3 reform closely.
2. Engage in public consultations and parliamentary processes.
3. Develop advocacy positions that emphasise the sui generis nature of self-custodied Bitcoin and the practical difficulties of enforcing unrealised gains taxation on it.

**Truth & Clarity Summary**  
The Netherlands’ planned 2028 tax reform poses one of the more significant risks in Europe to long-term self-custody through potential unrealised gains taxation. The sui generis and adjacent currency arguments are highly relevant for advocacy in this jurisdiction.
