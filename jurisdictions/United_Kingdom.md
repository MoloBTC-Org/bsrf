## Executive Summary

The United Kingdom has taken a relatively pragmatic approach to Bitcoin compared to some EU jurisdictions. Self-custody is generally respected, and taxation is on a realisation basis for individuals. The UK has developed its own regulatory framework post-Brexit, with the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) as the primary regulator.

Bitcoin is treated as a form of property under UK law for certain purposes (e.g., insolvency and tracing). There is no specific wealth tax or unrealised gains tax on crypto.

---

## 1. Historical Thread

The UK was relatively early in providing regulatory guidance on crypto. The FCA has issued guidance distinguishing between different types of cryptoassets. Post-Brexit, the UK has been developing its own regulatory perimeter, including rules for stablecoins, custody, and market abuse.

---

## 2. Current Regulatory Overview

- **Regulation**: Crypto businesses must register with the FCA for anti-money laundering purposes. Broader authorisation requirements apply depending on activities (e.g., operating a trading platform or providing custody).
- **Taxation**: Individuals are generally taxed on capital gains from crypto disposals. No specific long-term holding exemption. Crypto-to-crypto trades can be taxable events.
- **Self-custody**: Not restricted.
- **Property Status**: UK courts have recognised cryptoassets as property for the purposes of insolvency, tracing, and certain proprietary claims.

## 5. Philosophical & Legal Synthesis

UK common law has strong property protections. The sui generis nature of Bitcoin is increasingly recognised in case law, with cryptoassets treated as property for certain legal purposes. The framework developed in this project aligns well with UK legal traditions regarding property, privacy, and freedom from arbitrary state interference.

---

## 6. Adoption Implications

The UK currently offers a relatively pragmatic environment for self-custodial Bitcoin activity. However, ongoing regulatory development (particularly around custody and stablecoins) means continued engagement is important to ensure self-custody rights are protected.
