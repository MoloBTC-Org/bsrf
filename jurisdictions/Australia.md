## Executive Summary

Australia has advanced its digital asset regulatory framework with the publication of draft laws for digital asset and stablecoin regulation. The Australian Securities and Investments Commission (ASIC) has also updated guidance on the application of existing financial regulation to crypto businesses.

Bitcoin is treated as a digital asset / financial product in certain contexts. Self-custody is fully permitted.

---

## 1. Regulatory Framework

- **Corporations Amendment (Digital Assets Framework) Bill 2025**: Proposed legislation to create a dedicated framework for digital assets.
- **ASIC Guidance**: Updated guidance (INFO 225) clarifying the application of existing financial services regulation to crypto businesses.
- **Stablecoins**: First financial services license granted to a stablecoin issuer.
- **AML Oversight**: Tightened requirements, particularly for crypto ATMs.

---

## 2. Taxation

Australia taxes cryptocurrency as a CGT asset. Gains on disposal are subject to capital gains tax. Specific rules apply to crypto-to-crypto transactions and staking rewards. The ATO has provided relatively clear guidance on tax treatment.

---

## 3. Key Developments (2025–2026)

- Publication of draft digital asset legislation.
- Granting of the first stablecoin-related financial services license.
- Updated ASIC guidance on crypto businesses.
- Increased AML focus, particularly on crypto ATMs.

---

## 4. Key Players

- Australian Securities and Investments Commission (ASIC)
- Australian Taxation Office (ATO)
- Reserve Bank of Australia (research on digital currencies)

---

## 5. Philosophical & Legal Alignment

Australia’s evolving framework applies existing financial services concepts to digital assets while developing dedicated rules. Self-custody is fully supported. The approach is broadly compatible with the sui generis treatment of Bitcoin when the regulatory focus remains on financial services activities rather than restricting individual ownership.

---

## 6. Adoption Implications

Australia offers a developed regulatory environment with increasing clarity for digital assets. The combination of existing tax rules, upcoming dedicated legislation, and strong institutional infrastructure makes it a relevant jurisdiction in the Asia-Pacific region.

---

## Next Steps

1. Monitor the progress of the Digital Assets Framework legislation.
2. Track ASIC enforcement priorities and guidance updates.
3. Use Australia as a reference point for common law jurisdictions developing dedicated digital asset rules.

---

**Truth & Clarity Summary**  
Australia is advancing dedicated digital asset legislation while applying existing financial regulation. Self-custody is fully supported. The jurisdiction is developing a more structured framework for crypto assets alongside strong tax and AML oversight.
