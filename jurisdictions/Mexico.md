## Executive Summary

Mexico has a regulated but restrictive framework for cryptocurrency. While cryptoassets are legal for individuals and non-financial entities, banks and financial institutions face significant limitations on offering virtual asset-related services. The regulatory focus is on preventing illicit finance and maintaining financial stability.

Bitcoin is treated as a virtual asset. Self-custody is permitted for individuals and non-financial entities.

---

## 1. Regulatory Framework

- **Fintech Law (2018)**: Introduced the legal concept of virtual assets and granted the Central Bank broad powers over virtual asset operations within the financial system.
- **Banking Restrictions**: Financial institutions are generally prohibited from offering services directly related to virtual assets (with limited exceptions).
- **AML/CTF**: Virtual assets are considered vulnerable activities under anti-money laundering law, with enhanced obligations following 2025 reforms.

---

## 2. Taxation

Cryptocurrency gains are subject to capital gains tax in Mexico, with rates ranging from approximately 1.92% to 35% depending on the taxpayer’s income bracket. Transactions below certain thresholds may be exempt from reporting.

---

## 3. Key Developments (2025–2026)

- Amendments to the Anti-Money Laundering Law in 2025, updating obligations for virtual asset activities.
- Continued restrictions on banks engaging with virtual assets.
- Ongoing development of the regulatory perimeter for fintech and virtual assets.

---

## 4. Key Players

- Bank of Mexico (Banco de México)
- National Banking and Securities Commission (CNBV)
- Tax Administration Service (SAT)

---

## 5. Philosophical & Legal Alignment

Mexico’s framework permits individual use and self-custody of Bitcoin while imposing significant restrictions on traditional financial institutions. This creates a bifurcated environment. The treatment of virtual assets as a distinct category is compatible with the sui generis view, though the banking restrictions limit institutional integration.

---

## 6. Adoption Implications

Mexico has a significant grassroots crypto market despite regulatory constraints on the traditional financial sector. The environment supports individual self-custody but creates challenges for broader institutional adoption and integration with the banking system.

---

## Next Steps

1. Monitor further developments in AML rules and any easing of banking restrictions.
2. Track tax enforcement and reporting developments.
3. Use Mexico as a case study of restrictive banking rules alongside permitted individual use.

---

**Truth & Clarity Summary**  
Mexico permits individual use and self-custody of cryptocurrency but maintains significant restrictions on banks and financial institutions. The regulatory environment is evolving with enhanced AML obligations. Self-custody remains available to individuals and non-financial entities.
