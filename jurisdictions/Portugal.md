# Portugal Nation Document  
**Bitcoin Regulation, Self-Custody & Sui Generis Treatment**  
*(Created 23 June 2026)*

**Executive Summary**  
Portugal has historically been one of the more Bitcoin-friendly jurisdictions in Europe regarding taxation. For several years, crypto gains by individuals were not taxed. While some changes have been introduced, Portugal remains relatively favourable compared to many other European countries.

Self-custody is not restricted, and there is no wealth tax or unrealised gains tax on crypto.

**1. Historical Thread**  
Portugal gained attention in the crypto community for its favourable tax treatment. In recent years, the government has introduced some taxation on crypto, but it remains more lenient than many EU peers.

**2. Current Regulatory Overview**  
- **Taxation**: Crypto gains by individuals are generally taxed at a flat rate (currently around 28% for holdings under 365 days in some cases). Long-term holdings may have different treatment. Professional activity is taxed differently.
- Self-custody is respected.
- Regulatory focus is primarily on service providers.

**3. Key Players**  
- Portuguese Tax Authority
- Bank of Portugal
- Securities market regulator (CMVM)

**4. Platforms for Advocacy**  
- Public consultations on tax policy
- Engagement with government and industry groups

**5. Philosophical & Natural-Law Synthesis**  
Portugal’s historically favourable treatment aligns well with recognising Bitcoin as a sui generis adjacent currency. The framework supports maintaining and clarifying this position.

**Updated Model Language**  
Protective language supporting realisation-based taxation and self-custody is recommended.

**6. Adoption Implications**  
Portugal remains attractive for Bitcoin users and businesses. Clear policy support for Bitcoin’s unique characteristics would further strengthen its position.

**Truth & Clarity Summary**  
Portugal continues to be one of the more favourable European jurisdictions for Bitcoin, though recent tax changes have reduced some of its previous advantages. The sui generis framework supports a clear, favourable treatment.
