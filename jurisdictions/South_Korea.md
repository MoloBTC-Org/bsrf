# South Korea Nation Document  
**Bitcoin Regulation, Self-Custody & Sui Generis Treatment**  
*(Created 23 June 2026)*

**Executive Summary**  
South Korea has a significant retail crypto market and has developed a regulatory framework focused on investor protection and market integrity. Taxation on crypto gains was introduced in recent years.

Self-custody is permitted, though there have been discussions around reporting requirements.

**1. Historical Thread**  
South Korea has had periods of restrictive measures (including trading hour limits and bank account verification requirements) alongside strong retail interest in crypto.

**2. Current Regulatory Overview**  
- **Taxation**: 20% capital gains tax on crypto gains above a certain threshold (implementation has been delayed multiple times).
- **Regulation**: Strict licensing and AML requirements for service providers.
- **Self-custody**: Generally permitted.

**3. Key Players**  
- Financial Services Commission
- National Tax Service

**4. Platforms for Advocacy**  
- Government consultations
- Industry groups

**5. Philosophical & Natural-Law Synthesis**  
South Korea’s strong retail adoption shows clear demand for Bitcoin. The sui generis framework can help argue for tax policies that do not unduly penalise long-term holders.

**Updated Model Language**  
Clear and fair tax treatment with proper recognition of Bitcoin’s characteristics is recommended.

**6. Adoption Implications**  
South Korea remains an important Asian market. Balanced policy would support continued adoption.
