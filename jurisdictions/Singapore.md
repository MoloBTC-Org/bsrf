# Singapore Nation Document  
**Bitcoin Regulation, Self-Custody & Sui Generis Treatment**  
*(Created 23 June 2026)*

**Executive Summary**  
Singapore has positioned itself as a leading crypto hub in Asia with a clear and relatively innovation-friendly regulatory framework. The Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS) has taken a measured approach, focusing on investor protection while supporting responsible innovation.

Self-custody is fully supported, and there is no capital gains tax for individuals on crypto disposals.

**1. Historical Thread**  
Singapore has actively sought to become a blockchain and crypto hub. MAS has issued clear guidance on digital asset service providers and has been relatively open to Bitcoin and blockchain businesses.

**2. Current Regulatory Overview**  
- **Regulation**: Service providers are regulated under the Payment Services Act. MAS has a licensing regime for crypto activities.
- **Taxation**: Singapore does not impose capital gains tax on individuals. Corporate tax applies to businesses.
- **Self-custody**: Fully permitted and common.

**3. Key Players**  
- Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS)
- Inland Revenue Authority of Singapore (IRAS)

**4. Platforms for Advocacy**  
- MAS consultations
- Industry engagement

**5. Philosophical & Natural-Law Synthesis**  
Singapore’s pragmatic and business-friendly approach aligns well with the sui generis framework. The absence of capital gains tax for individuals makes it particularly attractive for long-term holders.

**Updated Model Language**

**6. Adoption Implications**  
Singapore is one of Asia’s most favourable jurisdictions for Bitcoin businesses and holders due to regulatory clarity and tax treatment.
