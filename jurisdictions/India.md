# India Nation Document  
**Bitcoin Regulation, Self-Custody & Sui Generis Treatment**  
*(Created 23 June 2026)*

**Executive Summary**  
India has had a volatile regulatory history with crypto, including a near-ban in 2018 that was later overturned. Currently, there is a 30% flat tax on crypto gains with very limited deductions, and a 1% TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) on transactions.

Self-custody is permitted but faces practical challenges due to the tax regime.

**1. Historical Thread**  
India’s regulatory approach has swung between restrictive and more open stances. The 2022 budget introduced significant taxation measures.

**2. Current Regulatory Overview**  
- **Taxation**: 30% flat tax on gains from "virtual digital assets" with very limited loss offsetting. 1% TDS on certain transactions.
- **Regulation**: The regulatory framework is still evolving. RBI has expressed concerns but has not banned crypto.
- **Self-custody**: Legally permitted but tax rules create significant friction.

**3. Key Players**  
- Central Board of Direct Taxes
- Reserve Bank of India
- Ministry of Finance

**4. Platforms for Advocacy**  
- Public consultations
- Industry associations and legal challenges

**5. Philosophical & Natural-Law Synthesis**  
India’s current tax regime is quite aggressive and does not distinguish well between different types of crypto assets. The sui generis framework is particularly relevant here to argue for more nuanced and fair treatment of Bitcoin.

**Updated Model Language**  
Strong arguments for distinguishing Bitcoin and moving toward realisation-based taxation with proper loss offsetting are needed.

**6. Adoption Implications**  
India has massive grassroots Bitcoin adoption potential, but the current tax regime is a significant headwind. Policy reform aligned with sui generis principles would be highly beneficial.

**Next Steps**  
- Monitor any policy review or reform discussions.
- Support advocacy efforts for fairer tax treatment.

**Truth & Clarity Summary**  
India’s current framework is one of the more challenging among major economies. The sui generis approach provides strong grounds for advocating reform.
