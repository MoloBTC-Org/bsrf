## Executive Summary

Tanzania currently has limited formal regulation specifically for cryptocurrency. The Bank of Tanzania has generally taken a cautious stance, advising against the use of virtual currencies while not imposing a blanket ban. Bitcoin is not legal tender.

Self-custody is not prohibited, though the lack of a clear regulatory framework creates uncertainty for businesses and institutional participants.

---

## 1. Regulatory Overview

- **No dedicated crypto law** currently in force.
- **Bank of Tanzania** has issued cautions regarding virtual currencies and maintains that the Tanzanian shilling is the only acceptable legal tender.
- **Self-Custody**: Not prohibited for individuals.
- **Business Activities**: Operate in a regulatory grey area with limited formal guidance.

---

## 2. Taxation

Specific tax treatment for cryptocurrency remains undeveloped. General tax principles would likely apply to any gains or business income derived from virtual assets.

---

## 3. Key Developments (2025–2026)

- Limited formal regulatory progress on cryptocurrency.
- Continued cautious stance from the central bank.
- Broader fintech regulatory developments ongoing, but crypto-specific rules remain limited.

---

## 4. Key Players

- Bank of Tanzania (BoT)
- Capital Markets and Securities Authority
- Tanzania Revenue Authority

---

## 5. Philosophical & Legal Alignment

The current environment in Tanzania permits individual self-custody in practice while maintaining regulatory caution at the institutional level. This creates both opportunities (freedom for individuals) and risks (uncertainty for businesses). The situation is less aligned with the framework’s preference for clear legal recognition of self-custody and sui generis treatment compared to jurisdictions with more developed frameworks.

---

## 6. Adoption Implications

Tanzania has shown grassroots interest in cryptocurrency, consistent with regional trends in East Africa. However, the absence of a clear regulatory framework limits institutional development and creates risks for businesses operating in the space.
