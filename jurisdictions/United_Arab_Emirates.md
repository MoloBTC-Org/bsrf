# United Arab Emirates Nation Document  
**Bitcoin Regulation, Self-Custody & Sui Generis Treatment**  
*(Created 23 June 2026)*

**Executive Summary**  
The UAE, particularly Dubai and Abu Dhabi, has positioned itself as one of the most crypto-friendly jurisdictions globally. It has clear regulatory frameworks (VARA in Dubai, ADGM in Abu Dhabi) and very favourable tax treatment (no personal income or capital gains tax).

Self-custody is fully supported, and the jurisdiction actively courts Bitcoin and blockchain businesses.

**1. Historical Thread**  
The UAE has aggressively pursued blockchain and crypto adoption as part of its economic diversification strategy. Dubai’s VARA and Abu Dhabi’s ADGM have created clear regulatory sandboxes and licensing regimes.

**2. Current Regulatory Overview**  
- **Regulation**: Clear licensing regimes through VARA (Dubai) and ADGM. Relatively innovation-friendly.
- **Taxation**: No personal capital gains or income tax on crypto for individuals.
- **Self-custody**: Fully supported.

**3. Key Players**  
- Dubai Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA)
- Abu Dhabi Global Market (ADGM)
- Central Bank of the UAE

**4. Platforms for Advocacy**  
- Direct engagement with regulators
- Industry events and associations

**5. Philosophical & Natural-Law Synthesis**  
The UAE’s approach is highly compatible with the sui generis framework. The absence of capital gains tax and strong support for self-custody and innovation make it one of the most favourable jurisdictions globally.

**Updated Model Language**  
Strong support for self-custody and clear distinction of Bitcoin is recommended.

**6. Adoption Implications**  
The UAE is currently one of the most attractive jurisdictions in the world for Bitcoin businesses, developers, and high-net-worth individuals.
