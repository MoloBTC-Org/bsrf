# Canada Nation Document  
**Bitcoin Regulation, Self-Custody & Sui Generis Treatment**  
*(Created 23 June 2026)*

**Executive Summary**  
Canada has a relatively clear and balanced regulatory environment for Bitcoin. It has been progressive in some areas (e.g., Bitcoin ETFs) while maintaining standard AML and tax rules. Self-custody is fully supported.

Taxation is on a realisation basis for individuals, with Bitcoin treated as a commodity.

**1. Historical Thread**  
Canada was relatively early in approving Bitcoin ETFs and has generally taken a pragmatic regulatory approach compared to some other jurisdictions.

**2. Current Regulatory Overview**  
- **Regulation**: Crypto service providers are subject to registration and AML requirements. Securities regulators have been active in the space.
- **Taxation**: Bitcoin is treated as a commodity. Capital gains rules apply to individuals (50% inclusion rate). No wealth tax or unrealised gains tax.
- **Self-custody**: Fully permitted.

**3. Key Players**  
- Canada Revenue Agency (CRA)
- Provincial securities regulators
- Bank of Canada (research interest)

**4. Platforms for Advocacy**  
- CRA and securities regulator consultations
- Industry associations

**5. Philosophical & Natural-Law Synthesis**  
Canada’s treatment of Bitcoin as a commodity is more coherent than security-based approaches. The sui generis framework can help refine policy further by recognising Bitcoin’s monetary characteristics.

**Updated Model Language**  
Clear distinction of Bitcoin and realisation-based taxation principles are recommended.

**6. Adoption Implications**  
Canada offers a reasonably predictable environment for Bitcoin. Further policy clarity would be beneficial.
