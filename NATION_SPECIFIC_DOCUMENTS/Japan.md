# Japan Nation Document  
**Bitcoin Regulation, Self-Custody & Sui Generis Treatment**  
*(Created 23 June 2026)*

**Executive Summary**  
Japan was one of the first major economies to recognise Bitcoin as a form of payment. It has a relatively mature regulatory framework focused on consumer protection and anti-money laundering, while generally supporting innovation.

Self-custody is permitted, and taxation is on a realisation basis for individuals.

**1. Historical Thread**  
Japan legalised Bitcoin as a payment method in 2017 following the Mt. Gox collapse, introducing strict licensing requirements for exchanges. The country has since developed one of Asia’s more established crypto regulatory regimes.

**2. Current Regulatory Overview**  
- **Regulation**: Crypto exchanges must be licensed. Strict AML and consumer protection rules apply.
- **Taxation**: Crypto gains are taxed as miscellaneous income for individuals (progressive rates up to 55% in some cases). No specific long-term holding benefit.
- **Self-custody**: Permitted.

**3. Key Players**  
- Financial Services Agency (FSA)
- National Tax Agency

**4. Platforms for Advocacy**  
- FSA consultations
- Industry associations

**5. Philosophical & Natural-Law Synthesis**  
Japan’s approach balances innovation with strong consumer protection. The sui generis framework can help argue for more nuanced treatment that distinguishes Bitcoin from other crypto assets.

**Updated Model Language**  
Clear distinction between Bitcoin and other crypto assets, along with realisation-based taxation principles, is recommended.

**6. Adoption Implications**  
Japan remains an important market in Asia. Further policy clarity on Bitcoin’s unique characteristics could support greater adoption.
