# Germany Nation Document  
**Bitcoin Regulation, Self-Custody & Sui Generis Treatment**  
*(Created 23 June 2026)*

**Executive Summary**  
Germany has one of the more favourable long-term tax treatments for Bitcoin in Europe: gains are generally **tax-free** if held for more than one year. This makes Germany relatively attractive for long-term holders.

Self-custody is respected, and there is no broad wealth tax or unrealised gains tax on crypto for individuals. Regulatory oversight focuses primarily on intermediaries through MiCA implementation.

**1. Historical Thread**  
Germany has been a significant European Bitcoin hub. The Federal Ministry of Finance has provided relatively clear guidance on tax treatment, with the one-year holding period being a key feature.

**2. Current Regulatory Overview**  
- **Tax Treatment**: Private individuals benefit from a **1-year holding rule** — gains on Bitcoin held longer than 12 months are completely tax-free. Disposals within 12 months are subject to personal income tax (0–45%) above a small exemption.
- Self-custody is not regulated.
- Intermediaries are subject to MiCA licensing and AML requirements.
- No wealth tax or unrealised gains tax on crypto for individuals.

**3. Key Players**  
- Federal Ministry of Finance (BMF)
- Federal Financial Supervisory Authority (BaFin)
- Federal Central Tax Office (BZSt)

**4. Platforms for Advocacy**  
- BMF and BaFin consultations
- Bundestag submissions on proposed legislation

**5. Philosophical & Natural-Law Synthesis**  
Germany’s 1-year exemption implicitly recognises long-term holding as distinct. The sui generis framework supports strengthening this treatment and resisting any future moves toward wealth-style taxation or exit taxes.

**Updated Model Language**  
Protective language maintaining the 1-year exemption and recognising sui generis status is recommended.

**6. Adoption Implications**  
Germany remains one of the more favourable major European jurisdictions for long-term Bitcoin holders due to the tax treatment. Maintaining and clarifying this position would support Bitcoin adoption.

**Next Steps**  
- Monitor any proposals to change the 1-year rule or introduce new taxes.
- Engage with German Bitcoin advocacy groups.

**Truth & Clarity Summary**  
Germany currently offers relatively favourable conditions for self-custodial, long-term Bitcoin holding. The sui generis framework aligns well with preserving and strengthening this position.
