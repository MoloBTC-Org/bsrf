# Brazil Nation Document  
**Bitcoin Regulation, Self-Custody & Sui Generis Treatment**  
*(Created 23 June 2026)*

**Executive Summary**  
Brazil has emerged as one of the most important Bitcoin markets in Latin America. It has developed a relatively clear regulatory framework and has seen strong grassroots adoption, including in the informal economy.

Self-custody is common and not restricted. Taxation applies to gains above certain thresholds.

**1. Historical Thread**  
Brazil has seen significant Bitcoin adoption, partly driven by economic instability and currency concerns. The government has moved to regulate the sector while generally allowing innovation.

**2. Current Regulatory Overview**  
- **Regulation**: Law 14.478/2022 established a regulatory framework for virtual assets. The Central Bank and CVM have roles in oversight.
- **Taxation**: Gains from crypto disposals above certain thresholds are taxed. Rules have been clarified in recent years.
- **Self-custody**: Fully permitted.

**3. Key Players**  
- Central Bank of Brazil
- Securities and Exchange Commission (CVM)
- Federal Revenue Service

**4. Platforms for Advocacy**  
- Public consultations
- Industry associations

**5. Philosophical & Natural-Law Synthesis**  
Brazil’s experience shows strong organic demand for Bitcoin as a hedge and alternative monetary instrument. The sui generis framework is highly relevant given the country’s history with currency issues.

**Updated Model Language**  
Clear recognition of Bitcoin’s monetary characteristics and protection of self-custody is recommended.

**6. Adoption Implications**  
Brazil is a major and growing Bitcoin market. Supportive policy that recognises Bitcoin’s unique nature would further strengthen adoption.

**Truth & Clarity Summary**  
Brazil has strong grassroots Bitcoin adoption and is developing a clearer regulatory framework. The sui generis approach aligns well with the country’s needs and adoption patterns.
