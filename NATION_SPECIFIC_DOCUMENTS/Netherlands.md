# Netherlands Nation Document  
**Bitcoin Regulation, Self-Custody & Sui Generis Treatment**  
*(Created 23 June 2026)*

**Executive Summary**  
The Netherlands is moving toward a significant shift in its tax treatment of assets, including crypto. Under current rules, crypto is taxed under Box 3 (wealth tax) based on deemed returns. Major reforms planned for 2028 will introduce taxation on actual returns, including unrealised gains for certain assets.

This development makes the Netherlands a key jurisdiction to watch regarding wealth-style taxation of Bitcoin and the tension with sui generis principles.

**1. Historical Thread**  
The Netherlands has historically had a relatively pragmatic approach to crypto within the EU context. It has been active in EU discussions on MiCA and tax policy.

**2. Current Regulatory Overview**  
- **Current Tax System (until 2027)**: Crypto taxed under Box 3 as “other investments” based on deemed yield.
- **2028 Reform**: Shift toward taxation on actual returns, potentially including unrealised gains. This creates significant tension with realisation-based and sui generis principles.
- **Self-custody**: Not directly restricted.

**3. Key Players**  
- Dutch Tax Authority (Belastingdienst)
- Ministry of Finance
- Netherlands Authority for the Financial Markets (AFM)

**4. Platforms for Advocacy**  
- Public consultations on Box 3 reform
- Engagement with Parliament and Ministry of Finance

**5. Philosophical & Natural-Law Synthesis**  
The planned 2028 changes represent a move toward wealth-style taxation that conflicts with the sui generis and realisation-based framework advocated in this project. Strong advocacy is needed to protect self-custodied Bitcoin from unrealised gains taxation.

**Updated Model Language**  
Strong protective language against taxation of unrealised gains and compelled disclosure is particularly important in the Dutch context.

**6. Adoption Implications**  
The Netherlands risks becoming less attractive for long-term Bitcoin holders if the 2028 reforms proceed without appropriate carve-outs for sui generis assets like Bitcoin.

**Next Steps**  
- Closely monitor Box 3 reform implementation.
- Engage with Dutch advocacy groups and policymakers.
- Use the supporting analyses to argue against unrealised gains taxation of self-custodied Bitcoin.

**Truth & Clarity Summary**  
The Netherlands is at a critical juncture. The 2028 tax reforms could significantly impact self-custodied Bitcoin holders. The sui generis framework provides strong grounds to advocate for carve-outs or alternative treatment.
