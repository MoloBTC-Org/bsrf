# Australia Nation Document  
**Bitcoin Regulation, Self-Custody & Sui Generis Treatment**  
*(Created 23 June 2026)*

**Executive Summary**  
Australia has a relatively clear regulatory and tax framework for crypto. Bitcoin is treated as a form of property for tax purposes, with gains taxed on realisation. Self-custody is not restricted.

Australia has been active in developing regulatory guidance and has generally taken a balanced approach between innovation and consumer protection.

**1. Historical Thread**  
Australia was relatively early in providing tax guidance on crypto (treating it as property since 2014). Regulatory focus has increased with the growth of the industry.

**2. Current Regulatory Overview**  
- **Taxation**: Crypto is treated as a CGT asset. Gains are taxed on disposal. There is no specific wealth tax or unrealised gains tax.
- **Regulation**: Service providers are subject to AML/CTF requirements and, in some cases, financial services licensing.
- **Self-custody**: Fully supported.

**3. Key Players**  
- Australian Taxation Office (ATO)
- Australian Securities and Investments Commission (ASIC)
- Reserve Bank of Australia (research interest)

**4. Platforms for Advocacy**  
- ATO and ASIC consultations
- Industry associations

**5. Philosophical & Natural-Law Synthesis**  
Australia’s treatment of crypto as property for tax purposes is more coherent than many jurisdictions. The sui generis framework can help refine this further by distinguishing Bitcoin’s monetary characteristics.

**Updated Model Language**  
Protective language supporting realisation-based taxation and clear distinction of Bitcoin is recommended.

**6. Adoption Implications**  
Australia offers a relatively predictable environment for Bitcoin users and businesses. Further clarity on Bitcoin’s unique status would be beneficial.

**Truth & Clarity Summary**  
Australia has a reasonably clear and workable framework. The sui generis approach can help improve policy clarity further.
