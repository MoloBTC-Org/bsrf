# Nigeria Nation Document  
**Bitcoin Regulation, Self-Custody & Sui Generis Treatment**  
*(Created 23 June 2026)*

**Executive Summary**  
Nigeria has one of the highest rates of grassroots Bitcoin and crypto adoption in Africa, driven by economic factors, currency controls, and a young, tech-savvy population. The Central Bank of Nigeria has taken a restrictive stance toward crypto, but adoption continues strongly in peer-to-peer markets.

Self-custody is widely practiced despite regulatory hostility from the central bank.

**1. Historical Thread**  
Nigeria has seen explosive growth in P2P crypto trading, particularly on platforms like Paxful and Binance P2P. The CBN has issued circulars restricting banks from dealing with crypto exchanges, but enforcement has been inconsistent.

**2. Current Regulatory Overview**  
- **Regulation**: The CBN has been hostile to crypto exchanges. However, the Securities and Exchange Commission has taken a more measured approach.
- **Taxation**: Specific crypto tax rules are still developing.
- **Self-custody**: Widely practiced and difficult to restrict.

**3. Key Players**  
- Central Bank of Nigeria
- Securities and Exchange Commission
- Active P2P trading community

**4. Platforms for Advocacy**  
- Community advocacy
- Legal challenges
- International pressure

**5. Philosophical & Natural-Law Synthesis**  
Nigeria is a powerful real-world example of Bitcoin adoption driven by economic necessity and distrust of central banking. It strongly validates the sui generis monetary thesis.

**Updated Model Language**  
Strong protection of self-custody and recognition of Bitcoin as an alternative monetary instrument is highly relevant.

**6. Adoption Implications**  
Nigeria shows that strong grassroots demand for Bitcoin exists even in the face of regulatory hostility. Policy that recognises this reality would be beneficial.

**Next Steps**  
- Monitor regulatory developments.
- Support community and legal advocacy efforts.

**Truth & Clarity Summary**  
Nigeria has very high organic Bitcoin adoption despite central bank opposition. It is a key case study in the power of Bitcoin as a monetary alternative.
