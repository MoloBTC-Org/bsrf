# Kenya Nation Document  
**Bitcoin Regulation, Self-Custody & Sui Generis Treatment**  
*(Created 23 June 2026)*

**Executive Summary**  
Kenya has significant grassroots crypto adoption, particularly in peer-to-peer trading and remittances. The regulatory environment is still developing, with the Central Bank of Kenya having expressed caution but not implementing a full ban.

Self-custody is common.

**1. Historical Thread**  
Kenya has seen strong mobile money adoption (M-Pesa) alongside growing crypto use. The intersection of these two has created interesting dynamics.

**2. Current Regulatory Overview**  
- **Regulation**: The Central Bank has warned against crypto but has not banned it. A more comprehensive framework is still developing.
- **Taxation**: Rules around crypto gains are still being clarified.
- **Self-custody**: Widely practiced.

**3. Key Players**  
- Central Bank of Kenya
- Capital Markets Authority
- Active crypto community

**4. Platforms for Advocacy**  
- Community advocacy
- Engagement with regulators

**5. Philosophical & Natural-Law Synthesis**  
Kenya’s experience shows Bitcoin’s potential in emerging markets with high remittance flows and currency challenges. The sui generis framework is highly relevant.

**Updated Model Language**  
Support for self-custody and recognition of Bitcoin’s role in financial inclusion is recommended.

**6. Adoption Implications**  
Kenya has strong adoption potential. Balanced policy would support further growth.
