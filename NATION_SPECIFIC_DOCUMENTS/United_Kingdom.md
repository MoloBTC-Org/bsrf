# United Kingdom Nation Document  
**Bitcoin Regulation, Self-Custody & Sui Generis Treatment**  
*(Created 23 June 2026)*

**Executive Summary**  
The United Kingdom has taken a relatively pragmatic approach to Bitcoin compared to some EU jurisdictions. Self-custody is generally respected, and taxation is on a realisation basis for individuals. The UK has its own regulatory framework post-Brexit, with the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) as the primary regulator.

Bitcoin is treated as a form of property under UK law for certain purposes (e.g., insolvency and tracing). There is no specific wealth tax or unrealised gains tax on crypto.

**1. Historical Thread**  
The UK was relatively early in providing regulatory guidance on crypto. The FCA has issued guidance on cryptoassets, distinguishing between different types. Post-Brexit, the UK has been developing its own regulatory perimeter for crypto.

**2. Current Regulatory Overview**  
- **Regulation**: Crypto businesses must register with the FCA for anti-money laundering purposes. Broader authorisation requirements apply depending on activities.
- **Taxation**: Individuals are generally taxed on capital gains from crypto disposals. No specific long-term holding exemption. Crypto-to-crypto trades can be taxable.
- **Self-custody**: Not restricted.

**3. Key Players**  
- Financial Conduct Authority (FCA)
- HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC)
- Bank of England (research and policy interest)

**4. Platforms for Advocacy**

- Parliamentary committees

**5. Philosophical & Natural-Law Synthesis**  
UK common law has strong property protections. The sui generis nature of Bitcoin is increasingly recognised in case law (e.g., crypto treated as property for certain legal purposes). The framework developed in this project aligns well with UK legal traditions.

**Updated Model Language**  
Protective language recognising Bitcoin as sui generis and limiting taxation to realised activity is recommended.

**6. Adoption Implications**  
The UK remains an important jurisdiction for Bitcoin. Clearer policy recognition of Bitcoin’s unique characteristics would support the development of Bitcoin-native infrastructure and services.

**Truth & Clarity Summary**  
The UK offers a relatively workable environment for self-custodial Bitcoin, though tax treatment can be more aggressive than pure realisation-based systems in some respects. The sui generis framework is highly applicable.
