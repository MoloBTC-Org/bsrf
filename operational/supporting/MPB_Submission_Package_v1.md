\### 1. Purpose of This Submission\
\
This package proposes targeted, high-value additions to the \*\*My First
Bitcoin (MPB)\*\* curriculum and GitHub resources. It focuses on
strengthening the \*\*self-custody security mindset\*\* and practical
sovereignty education --- areas that align directly with MPB's mission
of building Bitcoin literacy from first principles.\
\
The materials are drawn from the \*\*Bitcoin Self-Custody Research
Ecosystem\*\* (Tier 1 foundational papers + Tier 2 operational
companions) and are designed to be modular, auditable, and suitable for
integration into existing MPB modules or as standalone resources.\
\

\### 3. Proposed Content Additions\
\
\#### 3.1 Self-Custody Security Mindset Module (Recommended Core
Addition)\
\
\*\*Suggested placement:\*\* New section or expanded module after
"Bitcoin Basics" and before or alongside existing wallet introductions.\
\
\*\*Key Topics to Include:\*\*\
\
- The difference between \*\*custodial\*\* and \*\*self-custodial\*\*
Bitcoin (counterparty risk, reallocation risk, surveillance risk).\
- Why "not your keys, not your coins" is a technical and philosophical
statement.\
- Introduction to the concept of Bitcoin as \*sui generis\* bearer
property.\
- Enforcement limitations of centralised systems (with current 2026
regulatory examples).\
- The three layers of Bitcoin security: Protocol → Node → Keys.\
- Common failure modes in self-custody and how to mitigate them (without
inducing fear).\
\
\*\*Suggested Deliverable:\*\*\
A concise 4--6 page module written at MPB's educational tone, with clear
learning objectives and discussion questions.\
\
\-\--\
\
\#### 3.2 QuickStart Guide -- Sovereign Self-Custody (Ready for
Integration)\
\
This is a practical, step-by-step onboarding guide designed for
individuals and small organisations moving from custodial or
exchange-held Bitcoin into self-custody.\
\
\*\*Core Sections (already developed):\*\*\
\
1. \*\*Why Self-Custody Matters\*\* (risk framing + sovereignty
mindset)\
2. \*\*Minimum Viable Sovereign Setup\*\* (hardware, software, backup
strategy)\
3. \*\*Recommended Tooling Pathway\*\*\
- Sparrow Wallet + Bitcoin Knots/Core (self-hosted node)\
- 2-of-3 Multisig with open-source air-gapped signers (Krux / SeedSigner
/ Specter DIY)\
4. \*\*Key Ceremony & Inheritance Basics\*\*\
5. \*\*Common Pitfalls & How to Avoid Them\*\*\
6. \*\*Next Steps & Further Learning\*\* (cross-reference to MPB
curriculum and Tier 1/Tier 2 papers)\
\
\*\*Status:\*\* Fully written and ready for review/adaptation. Can be
included as a downloadable PDF or Markdown file within the MPB
repository.\
\
\-\--\
\
\#### 3.3 Condensed Risk Framework Overview\
\
A short briefing document that introduces organisational and individual
risk thinking using the \*\*ISA/IEC 62443\*\* defence-in-depth model
applied to monetary infrastructure.\
\
\*\*Value to MPB:\*\*\
Helps more advanced students and educators understand \*why\* certain
self-custody architectures are structurally safer than others, moving
beyond "use a hardware wallet" advice.\
\
