### Introduction: Why Self-Custody Matters

Holding Bitcoin on an exchange or with a custodian means trusting a third party. History shows exchanges can be hacked, go bankrupt, freeze accounts, or misuse funds.

Self-custody removes that dependency. When done correctly, it gives you direct, verifiable control with no intermediary that can freeze or lose your coins due to their failures.

**Core Principle**: Self-custody converts institutional risk into operational responsibility. The goal is appropriate, maintainable security.

---

### Minimum Viable Sovereign Setup

| Component | Recommendation | Purpose |
|-----------|----------------|---------|
| Wallet Software | Sparrow Wallet | Strong multisig & coin control |
| Bitcoin Node | Self-hosted Knots or Core | Independent verification |
| Signing Devices | 2-of-3 Multisig (Krux/SeedSigner/Specter DIY) | Air-gapped, no single point of failure |
| Backup | Geographically distributed + metal | Recovery without single location risk |

---

### Step-by-Step Implementation Pathway

1. Prepare environment (signing devices + Sparrow + local node)
2. Create 2-of-3 multisig wallet
3. Conduct documented Key Ceremony
4. Secure seed phrases (separate locations)
5. Test recovery before moving large amounts
6. Move funds in stages
7. Establish basic inheritance planning

---

### Key Ongoing Processes

- Regular verification
- Transaction discipline (PSBT review)
- Software updates (tested)
- Annual backup testing

---

### Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- All seeds in one location
- Single-sig for large holdings
- Moving large amounts without testing recovery
- Poor documentation

---

### Key Takeaways

- Self-custody removes counterparty risk.
- 2-of-3 multisig + air-gapped signing is strong for most users.
- Good processes matter as much as good tools.
- Start small, test thoroughly, scale gradually.
