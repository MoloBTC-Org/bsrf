## 4. Visual & Styling Standards

All future PDFs must adhere exactly to the following specifications. These standards are non-negotiable for visual cohesion.

| Element              | Specification |
|----------------------|---------------|
| Paper Size           | A4 Portrait |
| Margins              | 0.9 – 1.0 inch (recommended 0.9 inch) |
| Body Font            | Helvetica (or clean sans-serif) — 10.5 pt |
| Main Title           | Helvetica Bold, 16–17 pt, Navy (#1a365d) |
| Section Headings     | Helvetica Bold, 11–12 pt, Navy (#1a365d) |
| Accent Color         | Gold (#c9a227) for rules, subtitles, and highlights |
| Header               | Thin gold rule (0.5 pt) + abbreviated document title |
| Footer               | As defined in Section 3.2 |
| Truth & Clarity Boxes| Light gray background (#f5f5f5) with navy border |
| Line Spacing         | 1.25 – 1.35 (generous but professional) |

### 4.1 Front-Door & Title Page Standards (Mandatory for Tiered Collections)

To maintain maximum visual professionalism and reader clarity across the ecosystem, the following rules are mandatory:

- **Formal Title Page Required**: Every Tier collection (Tier 1 and Tier 2) must begin with a dedicated formal title page using the exact block in Section 3.1. The Executive Overview & Reading Guide follows immediately after.
- **Front-Door Density Target**: The Executive Overview & Reading Guide (front door) must be information-dense. Target: one well-structured page preferred; maximum two tightly formatted pages. Excessive vertical spacing or sparse pages are not permitted.
- **White Space Guidance**: Up to approximately one quarter of a page of white space before a new subheading is acceptable. Do not force content into an overly cramped layout.
- **Widow/Orphan Control**: Enable in all front-matter paragraphs and tables to prevent single lines at page breaks.

> **TRUTH & CLARITY — Front-Door Standard**  
> The front door is the first substantive content readers encounter after the title page. It must feel substantial, well-crafted, and welcoming — never sparse or rushed. This standard directly supports the ecosystem goal of maximum clarity and professional authority.

### 4.2 Cross-Tier Reference Standards

Consistent cross-tier language prevents reader confusion and reinforces ecosystem cohesion. Use the following phrasing standards:

- **Tier 2 referring to Tier 1**: “Tier 2 builds upon the foundational papers in Tier 1. Readers seeking deeper philosophical and jurisprudential grounding should consult Tier 1, particularly [specific paper title].”
- **Tier 1 synthesis referring to Tier 2 companions**: “This whitepaper is fully supported by three detailed operational companion documents: [list exact titles].”
- **Executive Overviews**: Both tiers must include a clear “How the Two Tiers Work Together” paragraph positioned after “Relationship to Tier 2”, using the approved wording.
- **Repetitive Executive Summaries**: Standalone whitepapers retain fuller Truth & Clarity boxes and depth. Collected Tier versions use tighter, navigational summaries with explicit cross-references to the standalone documents.

### 4.3 Front-Matter Formatting Rules

The following rules apply specifically to title pages and Executive Overview & Reading Guide documents:

- Line spacing in front matter: 1.25–1.30 (slightly tighter than body text for density).
- Paragraph spacing: 4–6 pt before/after for body text; 8–10 pt before section headings.
- After the formal title page, the Executive Overview must begin on the next page with no large empty region.
- The “How the Two Tiers Work Together” paragraph must be positioned cleanly after “Relationship to Tier 2” with no page break interrupting the logical flow of the front door.
