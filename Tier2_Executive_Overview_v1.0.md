## Wamkelekile / Welcome

This document serves as the front door to the Tier 2 operational collection within the Bitcoin Self-Custody Research Ecosystem. Its purpose is to help readers quickly understand the structure of the materials and find the most relevant entry point for their needs.

### Purpose of This Research

The overall aim of this body of work is to enable ease of communication and collaboration between individuals and teams working on Bitcoin self-custody, sovereignty, risk management, and implementation. It provides both high-level strategic foundations and detailed operational guidance.

### Document Structure – Tier 2

Tier 2 consists of three operational companion documents designed to be used together:

1. **Centralised Monetary Governance** — Historical patterns of centralised monetary power and their modern implications.
2. **Bitcoin Self-Custody as a Strategic Risk-Mitigation Framework for Organisational Treasury** — Risk analysis, counterparty exposure, and mitigation strategies.
3. **Bitcoin Self-Custody as Critical Monetary Infrastructure** — Training, tooling, implementation pathways, and real-world validation.

### Navigation Documents

- This Executive Overview & Reading Guide — Primary front door for Tier 2.
- Quick Reference & Navigation Card — Located at the back of this collection for quick orientation.

### How to Use This Collection

**Recommended Reading Paths**

- **Treasury & Finance Teams**: Start with the Risk-Mitigation Framework, then move to Critical Monetary Infrastructure.
- **IT, Security & Operations Teams**: Begin with Critical Monetary Infrastructure (primary document), supported by the Risk-Mitigation Framework.
- **Board, Executives & Strategy**: Read this Overview first, then the Risk-Mitigation Framework for high-level understanding.
- **First-time readers**: Start here, then follow the path most relevant to your role.

### Relationship to Tier 1

Tier 2 builds upon the foundational papers in Tier 1. Readers seeking deeper philosophical and jurisprudential grounding should consult Tier 1, particularly the updated historical synthesis in Section 1 of *Bitcoin Self-Custody as Sovereign Monetary Infrastructure*.
